STOP 02/12 · FIRST LANDING

NEW YORK

Hello — new alphabet, same ambition.

We landed here from Bombay when I was small: the biggest city in the world, seen from knee height. Everything moved. Everyone was building something.

It's also where markets stopped being abstract — my dad worked in finance, and the numbers came home in dinner-table stories. Some part of me started keeping score early. That thread runs through everything ahead: the investment clubs, the trading desk, the VC research.

First landing. First taste of the game.

STOP 03/12 · THE DETOUR THAT STUCK · MIDDLE SCHOOL

McALLEN

Bienvenidos — the first time Spanish was a tool, not a class.

A middle-school service trip to the Rio Grande Valley. I expected a week of community service. I got a crash course in what a border actually is: families mid-paperwork, kids my age translating for their parents, systems that worked better on paper than in person.

It rearranged something. Immigration stopped being just my family's story and became something I wanted to work on.

Hold that thought until Washington — this seed grows into IROC. →
